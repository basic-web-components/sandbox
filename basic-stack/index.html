Below you see a basic-stack element containing an image and some text. The basic-stack has a semi-transparent background so you can see the element's bounds. As you resize the window, note that the basic-stack updates its size to be the maximium height and width of its two children. Significantly, the source does not require that the heights of those child elements be pre-calculated at design time and fixed in CSS.
